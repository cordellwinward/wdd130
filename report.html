Client & Developer Report

CLient

As the client for this project I designed my site plan expecting someone else to be developing this project. By so doing, I tried to make the design relatively simple and formatted such that the CSS could mostly be transferred to the child pages. I also was detailed about what information I wanted my site to have, finding the exact pages and text to best fit the context of my page. Most of my images were created by AI, often referencing pieces of pottery I built while in high school, and were chosen to be relevant to the text they referenced. I primarily communicated with my developer through teams, and answered some minor questions concerning my site, along with having a detailed plan to make most of my team. My lead developer met my site expectations in some ways but could have improved in others. The home page generally looks professional, and I was impressed with how they used my color scheme and fonts, along with their design of the navigation. However, some parts of the body did not completely follow the design specified in my wireframe. Additionally, one of my child pages is completely missing and the other one, while it also appears professional, is not uniform with the styling of the home page. I think that if I were to do this assignment again, I would have more actively given feedback on the sites progress than I did.

Senior Developer-Preston's Art Gallery

During this project acting the lead developer on my team, I actively communicated with my team and gave my team members assignments on teams. Near the beginning of the project, I made sure that I had invited my junior developers to my GitHub repo and asked them to let me know when they joined, along with giving them their child page assignments. Despite that, for most of the project, they did not respond to my messages in teams, and both sub-pages for the site are completely empty. I believe the site plan that we were building off also contributed to the difficulties in building the site, especially the child pages. I would communicate with my client in class and over teams consistently. At the beginning of the project, I went to my client and was able to get the wire frame and some basic information about the site and the child page content, but I never super clear information about the exact content of the child pages, possibly contributing to the child pages never being created. Overall, managing the site on GitHub was relatively easy.

Junior Developer-Project 8

Junior Developer Project 10

Overall as a junior developer, things went fairly smoothly. I was able to communicate with my team leads on teams and in class and generally knew what was expected of me and I was able to build the pages I was assigned. I wrote the HTML for my assigned pages, and created supplemental CSS files for the subpages to make any changes I needed to the home page styling without causing conflicts with multiple people editing the same file at the same time. My pages are complete with exception to a couple of little details that should maybe be changed do to a lack of information in the site plan where I never got updates content from my clients that I could not resolve. I had some minor issues with merging my contributions to GitHub but was able to resolve them fairly easily.

General

I expected this project to be more complicated than it was. Initially, when I saw this project, I thought that it would be difficult working on 3 different web sites at the same time, but I discovered that the workload wasn’t as bad as I thought it would be. I enjoyed being able to build the website and work with other people’s styles to adapt them as needed to best suit the pages I was responsible for. The most challenging part of this project was just communicating with my team and having people respond with the additional information I needed to build their website. Across the pages I worked on, there are some minor content errors due to communication. If I were to work on this project again, I would probably just focus on communicating better, likely setting up group chats outside of teams in order to improve communication channels. After working on this site, I feel more comfortable working on real world projects just because of the experience this has given me with working in a team and how to effectively do so. I believe this project has made it easier for me to better communicate with my team in the future while I am working on real world projects.
